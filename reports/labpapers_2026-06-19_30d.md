#### [Measuring LLMs’ impact on N-day exploits](https://www.anthropic.com/research/n-days)
Winnie Xiao, Tim Abbott, Nicholas Carlini, Newton Cheng, David Forsythe, Keane Lucas, Milad Nasr, Shikhar Sakhuja
- date 2026-06-08 | signal: max author citations 4349, sum 5211, paper citations 0 | category Frontier Red Team | via anthropic-site
- affiliation evidence: https://www.anthropic.com/research/n-days
- [abs](https://www.anthropic.com/research/n-days)

> For the last few months, we’ve been writing about large language models’ cybersecurity capabilities. For the most part, we’ve focused on zero-days—vulnerabilities that are unknown to the software’s maintainers. But a large fraction of real-world harm comes from N-days : vulnerabilities that have already been publicly disclosed, but only patched on some devices. Attackers exploit the many systems that haven't yet applied the patch, during what’s known as the “patch gap.”

#### [Mapping AI-enabled cyber threats: Insights from the LLM ATT&CK Navigator](https://www.anthropic.com/research/attack-navigator)
Kyla Guru, Alex Moix, Jacob Klein
- date 2026-06-03 | signal: max author citations 226, sum 226, paper citations 0 | category Frontier Red Team | via anthropic-site
- affiliation evidence: https://www.anthropic.com/research/attack-navigator
- [abs](https://www.anthropic.com/research/attack-navigator)

> We’ve spent the past year investigating how threat actors are weaponizing AI to conduct cyber operations. Today, we’re sharing a new analysis that maps these real-world attacks onto the MITRE ATT&CK® framework , a database of tactics and techniques used by cyberattackers. Doing so reveals patterns that challenge traditional assumptions about cybersecurity—for example, the level of risk a threat actor poses can be assessed via metrics like technical sophistication or breadth of techniques. We partnered with Verizon to include some of these results in the 2026 Verizon Data Breach Investigation R

> As a result, coding agents have advanced much more quickly than biological agents.
